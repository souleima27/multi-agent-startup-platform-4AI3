# Startup Final Report

## 1. Startup summary
- What the startup is: A smart cold-chain rental network in Tunisia that provides connected refrigerated boxes and pickup lockers for fishermen, food producers, and small distributors to move perishable goods safely during same-day transport.
- What problem it solves: Small food businesses often lose money because they cannot maintain a reliable cold chain during transport and temporary storage, especially for fish, dairy, and fresh produce.
- Who it serves: Fishermen, small food producers, and local distributors in Tunisia, especially in coastal cities.
- How it works: Businesses rent connected refrigerated boxes and use pickup lockers plus a simple dashboard to track temperature and manage same-day perishable transport.

## 2. Market existence and innovation view
- The idea appears to offer something different by specifically targeting perishable goods transport with connected refrigeration, whereas existing companies don't provide cold-chain rental networks.
- Relevant competitors or similar companies in Tunisia include Kamioun (B2B supply chain solutions for retailers, but not cold-chain rentals) and JPARTNERS TUNISIE (business partnerships firm with no direct relevance).
- The idea seems innovative for a startup, as it addresses a niche pain point in Tunisia's food supply chain with a tech-enabled rental model.

## 3. Recommended MVP
- Core MVP summary: A rental network of solar-powered refrigerated lockers with temperature sensors and cellular connectivity, enabling Tunisian fishermen and distributors to maintain cold chains during same-day transport.
- Main must-have features:
  - Hardware: Refrigerated locker with temperature sensors, solar battery, and SIM card for cellular connectivity.
  - Software: Dashboard for real-time temperature monitoring, alert notifications, and device status tracking.
  - Workflow: Customer rents locker, installs SIM card, activates via app, uses for transport/storage, receives alerts for temperature deviations.
- Main user journey:
  - Fisherman rents locker from distributor.
  - Installer inserts SIM card and activates device via app.
  - User loads perishables into locker.
  - Sensors monitor temperature and send alerts if thresholds are breached.
  - User retrieves product or uses pickup locker for deliveries.
  - System logs data for compliance and quality tracking.
- What should stay out of scope initially: Advanced analytics, AI predictions, expansion to non-coastal areas, integration with third-party logistics systems.
